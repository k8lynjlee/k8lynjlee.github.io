Involvement
University of Southern California
USC Association for Computing Machinery (ACM)
December 2012 - Present

I am currently serving my third term as USC ACM’s Communications Directory to design and distribute our organization’s weekly newsletters, promote ACM’s events including tech talks, info sessions, hackathons, programming contests, and social gatherings, and manage USC ACM’s social media accounts. I enjoy working behind the scenes with E-board members and industry representatives to organize events to help students develop their technical and professional development skills, and mentoring younger students in computer science.

USC Society of Women Engineers (SWE)
Fall 2012 - Present

I have been a board member and ambassador for SWE USC on the Community Affairs Committee since my freshman year. Our committee creates and executes workshops and events to introduce local K-12 students to engineering disciplines. I enjoy teaching engineering workshops to Girl Scout Brownies and Juniors and mentoring high school students through our many events each semester, as I am very passionate about helping girls become interested in engineering and technology. I have led Programming in Python workshops for girls of all ages, including at SWE USC’s Introduce a Girl to Engineering Day with middle school girls. It is incredible to see student’s enthusiasm as they learn how to code.

USC Ballroom Dance Team
Fall 2013 - Present

I have enjoyed competing in collegiate ballroom competitions since Fall 2013 through the USC Ballroom Dance Team. I served as the secretary for the team from 2014-2015.

DoSomething.org Youth Advisory Council
Youth Advisory Council Member, 2008 - 2010 term

My involvement with non-profit DoSomething.org as one of thirty Youth Advisory members, and currently the alumni group, was an incredible opportunity to meet teens from all over the country to discuss issues that affect youth today. Though monthly conference calls and trips to New York and Los Angeles, I learned how to write grants and carry out projects, brainstormed ideas for youth campaigns, and reviewed grants for approval. I have been empowered to make a difference in my community. I educated students about tough issues such as dating violence and bullying, rallied students to advocate for music education, and organized projects such as Teens for Jeans to help the homeless and a jazz band that performed at a retirement center.
